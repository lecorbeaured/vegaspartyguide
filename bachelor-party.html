Bachelor Party FAQs

8–14 people is the sweet spot. Large enough for a party atmosphere, small enough to get into clubs together and split cabs/Ubers easily. Groups over 20 become difficult to manage and coordinate.

Upscale casual to dressy. Wear dress shoes or clean sneakers, slacks or dark jeans, and a collared shirt or nice button-down. NO athletic wear, no cargo shorts, no hats. If in doubt, overdress — you'll never get turned away for looking too good.

For bachelor parties, a table is usually worth it — especially for groups of 6+. Table minimums typically range from $500–$2,000+ depending on the club and night, but you get bottle service, dedicated space, and priority entry. Split among the group it's often not much more than cover + drinks at the bar.

Budget $800–$1,500 per person for a 3-day trip (not including flights). That covers hotel ($150–$300/night), food ($100/day), nightclub entry + drinks ($150–$300/night), and experiences like the helicopter tour. Add a gambling budget on top if you plan to play.

Spring (March–May) and Fall (September–November) are ideal — great weather, top DJ lineups, and active pool season. Avoid July–August (scorching heat, 110°F+) and major holiday weekends unless you book far in advance and enjoy massive crowds.
