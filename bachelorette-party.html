The Perfect 3-Day Vegas Bachelorette Weekend

A luxurious, unforgettable itinerary for the bride and her girls

1

Day 1 — Arrival, Spa & First Night Out

Glam up, relax, and kick off the celebration

12:00 PM

Hotel Check-In & Welcome Bubbly

Check into your Strip hotel. Arrange rose gold balloons, sashes, and a welcome bottle of champagne for the bride's arrival. Set the tone from the moment you walk in.

2:00 PM

Spa Day at Qua Baths & Spa — Caesar's Palace TREAT YOURSELVES

Award-winning spa with Roman baths, arctic ice room, and signature treatments. Book group packages for the full experience. The bride should get a massage — non-negotiable.

7:30 PM

VIP Dinner: Nobu at Caesar's or SW Steakhouse at Wynn

Celebrate in style. Dress up, bring the sashes, and make it a moment. Most restaurants will do something special for bachelorette groups — ask when you reserve.

10:30 PM

Omnia Nightclub at Caesar's Palace

The most glamorous nightclub in Vegas. Multi-level with a main room, intimate spaces, and a rooftop terrace. Book a table for the bride's group — you'll get special treatment.

2

Day 2 — Pool Party, Sphere & VIP Night

The main event — the day the bride will never forget

11:00 AM

Pool Party at Venus Pool Club or Encore Beach Club

Vegas pool parties are legendary. Venus Pool Club at Caesar's is women-only and intimate; Encore Beach Club is a full dayclub production. Book cabanas early for the premium experience.

4:00 PM

The Sphere Experience BOOK THIS

The Wizard of Oz in 18K resolution on the world's largest immersive entertainment dome. An experience unlike anything on earth — and the perfect bachelorette party story to tell for years.

Book on GetYourGuide — From $79
8:00 PM

VIP Dinner: Gordon Ramsay Hell's Kitchen or Spago

Treat the bride to a world-class meal. Champagne, amazing food, and a stunning Strip setting. Make a reservation at least 2 weeks in advance for weekends.

11:00 PM

XS Nightclub at the Encore

The biggest night of the weekend. XS is consistently ranked the #1 club in the world. Outdoor pool deck, world-class DJs, and an incredible atmosphere. This is the main event.

3

Day 3 — Champagne Brunch & Farewell

Send off the bride in style

11:00 AM

An over-the-top Vegas brunch to close out the weekend. Bottomless bubbles, incredible food, and time to relive the best moments of the trip together. The perfect farewell for the bride.

High Tea at the Wynn

An elevated, feminine experience — finger sandwiches, artisan teas, and desserts in a stunning setting. A beautiful, memorable moment for the bride and her crew before heading home.

5:00 PM

Farewell & Safe Travels

Exchange numbers, share photos, and say goodbye with the biggest smiles. The bride is one step closer to her forever — and she'll never forget this weekend.

Bachelorette Party FAQs

Budget $800–$1,500 per person for a 3-day Vegas bachelorette trip. This covers hotel ($169–$329/night), spa treatments ($150+), dinner ($80–$150/person), nightclub table split, and experiences like The Sphere ($79). The bride typically doesn't pay — her expenses are split among the group.

6–12 people is ideal. Big enough to rent a limo and get a club table, small enough to move around easily and keep everyone together. Groups of 15+ can become hard to coordinate at restaurants and clubs. Consider a smaller inner circle of close friends.

Dress to impress — Vegas clubs love a well-dressed group. The bride usually wears white; the crew coordinates in black, rose gold, or a fun color. Heels or dressy flats, stylish outfits, and matching sashes or accessories make it clear it's a bachelorette party. Most clubs give brides special treatment when it's obvious!

Yes — many Strip hotels offer special bachelorette packages that include room upgrades, welcome amenities (champagne, balloons, chocolates), spa credits, and restaurant reservations. Call the hotel directly to ask about bachelorette packages before booking. Caesar's, Wynn, and Bellagio are particularly good at this.

Vegas has amazing non-club options: The Sphere immersive experience, spa days at Qua or Canyon Ranch, high tea at the Wynn, champagne picnics at Red Rock Canyon, cooking classes at Gordon Ramsay's, helicopter tours over the Strip, High Roller observation wheel, and gondola rides at the Venetian. There's no shortage of daytime elegance.
